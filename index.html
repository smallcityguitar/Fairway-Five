How to Play

Guess the mystery DGPT pro in 5 tries or fewer. Pick MPO, FPO, or Mixed Field — everyone playing that division on the same day gets the same puzzle.

You start with nothing but a flight path. Each wrong guess reveals one hint, randomly chosen from:

  • Hometown
  • C1X Putting %
  • Best Major/Elite Series finish this year
  • Current Pro Tour standing
  • Current PDGA rating
  • Major/Elite Series events played this season
  • Average Major/Elite Series finish this season
  • PDGA member since (actual join year)

There are 8 possible hints. You get one for free right away, before your first guess, so you're not starting completely blind — in Normal mode that starter hint will never be Hometown, Best Major/Elite Series Finish, or Current Pro Tour Standing (those are fair game later, just not as the free opener). Four more get revealed one at a time on wrong guesses 1–4. Your 5th guess, if you need it, is your last shot with no new hint attached.

Example round:

Stroke 1 — Teeing Off: guess "Paul McBeth" → miss → hint: Hometown: Huntington Beach, CA
Stroke 2 — In The Fairway: guess "Gannon Buhr" → miss → hint: C1X Putting %: 90.9%
Stroke 3 — Approaching: guess "Richard Wysocki" → correct! → "Throw In!" — scored 3/5

Only the current top 40 MPO and top 40 FPO Tour standings are included as possible answers, though all of them are guessable regardless. Use "Copy Result" after a round to share your score as an emoji grid.

Privacy — what's stored/shared

What's Stored & Shared

On your device only (never sent anywhere): which player you're guessing each day, your guesses, win/loss history, personal stats and streaks, and your colorblind-mode preference. This all lives in your browser's own storage — not tied to your name, not sent to me, and gone if you clear your browser data. There are no accounts or sign-ins.

Shared anonymously, for the "how everyone did" histogram: when you finish a puzzle, this app sends the date, division/difficulty, and whether you won (and in how many guesses) — no name, no device ID, nothing that identifies you. It's added to a simple running count (e.g. "14 people finished in 3 guesses today"). There's no record of who specifically got what.

What I (the developer) can see: only those same anonymous aggregate counts — never which specific player you were guessing, and never anything tied to you personally.

Third-party bits: this page loads fonts from Google Fonts, which is standard practice but does mean Google's servers see a request when the page loads, same as most websites. Hosting (Vercel) may also keep basic technical logs like IP addresses briefly for security/operational purposes — that's just how web hosting works generally, not something this app adds on top.

No ads, no ad trackers, no analytics beyond what's described above.
